Custom Tile Work

Tile That Elevates Every Room It Touches

Tile is one of the most durable and visually impactful finishes in any home. Coastal Peak Renovations provides custom tile installation for kitchens, bathrooms, showers, and floors — delivering results that are clean, precise, and built to hold up through daily use.

Whether you are updating a dated kitchen backsplash, retiling a shower that has seen better days, or laying new floor tile across a bathroom or entryway, proper installation makes all the difference. Poorly set tile cracks, shifts, and collects moisture in the wrong places. Our work is done with the attention to layout, leveling, and grouting that professional results require.

We work with homeowners across Hampton Roads and Greater Richmond on tile projects of all sizes — from a single bathroom accent wall to a full shower tile overhaul. Our team brings the same care to every project regardless of scope, and we communicate clearly about materials, timeline, and what to expect throughout the process.

Tile done well adds real value and lasting visual appeal to a home. Tile done poorly becomes a problem that has to be fixed. Coastal Peak Renovations is committed to getting it right the first time.

Common Homeowner Concerns

Signs Your Tile May Need Attention

Cracked or Loose Tiles

Tiles that have cracked, shifted, or come loose from the substrate are a sign of age, poor installation, or moisture intrusion that needs to be addressed.

Stained or Failing Grout

Grout that is discolored, crumbling, or pulling away from tile edges creates openings for moisture and makes surfaces look worn and neglected.

Outdated Bathroom or Kitchen Tile

Tile that no longer fits the look and feel of your home can make an otherwise updated space feel dated and unfinished.

Shower Tile Deterioration

Shower tile that is cracking, staining, or showing signs of moisture damage behind the surface can lead to more significant structural concerns if not addressed.

No Kitchen Backsplash

A kitchen without a backsplash leaves walls exposed to moisture, grease, and staining that is difficult to clean and reduces the overall appearance of the space.

Uneven or Poorly Laid Floors

Floor tile that was not set level or was installed over an inadequate substrate tends to crack and shift over time, creating both aesthetic and safety issues.
