Door Installation

Upgrade Your Doors for Security, Appearance, and Performance

Doors affect security, appearance, energy performance, and the overall look and feel of the home. Coastal Peak Renovations provides door installation services to help homeowners across Hampton Roads and Greater Richmond upgrade worn, outdated, or underperforming doors.

Entry doors and exterior doors take a lot of punishment over the years. They are exposed to weather, used daily, and expected to seal the home against drafts, moisture, and the elements. When a door begins to fail in any of these functions — whether through age, damage, poor sealing, or worn hardware — it is time to consider replacement.

A new door does more than just look better. It provides improved security, better insulation against outdoor temperatures, and a cleaner, more finished appearance for both the interior and exterior of the home. The right door in the right opening makes a noticeable difference in how the home feels and performs.

We work with homeowners to select and install doors that fit the opening, the home's style, and the goals of the project — with professional installation that ensures proper sealing, alignment, and long-term performance from day one.

Common Homeowner Concerns

Signs Your Doors May Need Replacement

Aging Entry Doors

Exterior doors that have aged past their useful life and are showing wear in the finish, frame, or overall structure.

Poor Sealing

Gaps around the door frame that allow drafts, moisture, or pests into the home — affecting comfort and efficiency.

Outdated Appearance

A door that looks dated relative to the rest of the home's exterior and is affecting overall curb appeal.

Reduced Security

An older door with a weakened frame, worn hardware, or structural issues that reduces the security of the home's entry points.

Difficult to Use

Doors that stick, swing incorrectly, or do not latch properly and have become a source of daily inconvenience.

Water Damage Around the Frame

A failed seal or damaged threshold can allow water intrusion around the door frame — leading to rot, swelling, and structural damage that worsens without a proper replacement.
