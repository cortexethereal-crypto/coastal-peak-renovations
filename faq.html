Siding, Windows & Doors

Siding replacement in Virginia typically ranges from $8,000 to $30,000 or more depending on the square footage of the home, material choice, and the condition of the existing sheathing underneath. Vinyl falls on the lower end, fiber cement runs toward the higher end and beyond. Homes with moisture damage or rotted sheathing add to the total. The best way to get an accurate number is a free exterior assessment — every home is different.
Window replacement typically ranges from $5,000 to $25,000 or more for a full home depending on window count, frame material, glass package, and style. Standard double-hung vinyl windows fall on the lower end, while casement, bay, and specialty windows in fiberglass run toward the higher end and beyond. The best way to get an accurate number is a free assessment — every home is different.
Door installation costs typically range from $1,500 to $12,000 or more depending on the number of doors, material, style, and whether framing or trim needs to be replaced. A single fiberglass entry door falls on the lower end, while a full exterior door replacement with patio doors and custom hardware runs toward the higher end and beyond. The best way to get an accurate number is a free walkthrough estimate.
Yes. Replacing old single-pane windows and worn exterior doors with modern double-pane low-E glass and insulated door units can reduce heating and cooling costs noticeably — especially in older Virginia homes with drafty frames and worn weatherstripping.

Radon Mitigation

Radon mitigation in Virginia typically ranges from $1,200 to $3,500 or more depending on the size of the home, foundation type, number of suction points required, and accessibility of the installation area. Homes with simple slab foundations fall on the lower end, while larger homes or complex layouts requiring multiple suction points run toward the higher end and beyond. The best way to get an accurate number is a free assessment — every home is different.
The EPA recommends mitigation when indoor radon levels are at or above 4 pCi/L. Levels between 2 and 4 pCi/L should be considered for mitigation, especially in homes with children. A certified radon test is the only way to know your home's level.
We install a sub-slab depressurization system — drilling suction points through the foundation slab, inserting a pipe, and connecting a continuously running fan that draws radon gas from beneath the home and vents it safely outside above the roofline before it can enter your living space.
Most installations are completed in 4 to 8 hours in a single day. The system is operational immediately after installation.
Yes. Virginia has elevated radon risk particularly in central and western regions, but Hampton Roads and Richmond area homes test high regularly. Radon is the second leading cause of lung cancer in the US after smoking — behind only cigarettes.
Yes. We recommend retesting 24 to 48 hours after installation and again 90 days later to confirm the system is performing correctly. Most systems reduce radon levels by 80 to 99 percent.
