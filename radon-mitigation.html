Radon Mitigation

Protecting Your Home from a Risk You Cannot See or Smell

Radon is a naturally occurring radioactive gas that forms when uranium in soil and rock breaks down. It is colorless, odorless, and tasteless — which makes it impossible to detect without a test. The EPA estimates that radon is the second leading cause of lung cancer in the United States, and homes across Virginia, including those in Hampton Roads and the Greater Richmond area, can have elevated levels.

Coastal Peak Renovations installs EPA-compliant radon mitigation systems designed to reduce radon levels to safe concentrations. The most effective and widely used method is sub-slab depressurization, a system that draws radon gas from beneath the home's foundation and routes it safely outside before it can enter the living space. When properly installed, these systems work continuously and quietly in the background — and they work well.

The process starts with testing. Knowing your home's radon level tells you whether a mitigation system is needed and provides a baseline for confirming the system's effectiveness after installation. If your home tests high, we design a system appropriate for your foundation type and layout, install it correctly, and verify the results.

Whether you are buying a home, selling a home, or simply want to know that your family's indoor air is safe, Coastal Peak Renovations provides radon mitigation services for homeowners across Hampton Roads and Greater Richmond. Common Homeowner Situations

When Homeowners Seek Radon Mitigation

Elevated Test Results

A radon test result at or above 4 pCi/L — the EPA action level — indicates that a mitigation system should be installed to reduce levels in the home.

Home Sale or Purchase

Radon testing is common during real estate transactions. Elevated levels often require a mitigation system as a condition of sale or as a protective measure for the buyer.

No Prior Testing

Many homeowners have never tested for radon. Any home — new or old, with or without a basement — can have elevated levels, and testing is the only way to know.

Failed or Aging System

Radon fans have a service life. An older or failing system may no longer be performing effectively and could require inspection, repair, or replacement.

Basement or Crawlspace Concerns

Homes with basements or unconditioned crawlspaces are common entry points for radon. Changes to those spaces can affect how radon moves through the home.

Health and Peace of Mind

Many homeowners pursue radon testing and mitigation simply to confirm their indoor air quality is safe for their family — especially in homes where children spend significant time at ground level.
